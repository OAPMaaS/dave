# OmniAccess — Brand Name & Logo Standard

Rules for writing the company name and using the logo. These protect brand
consistency across all corporate documents.

## Writing the company name

- Always write the name as **OmniAccess** — capital **O**, lower-case "mni",
  capital **A**, lower-case "ccess". Capitals for the whole word are allowed **only**
  in the logo or in a headline.
- The name is used **without any article, prefix or suffix** — refer to the company
  simply as "OmniAccess".
- The name **cannot be written in the plural** ("OmniAccesses" is wrong).
- The possessive form is allowed (e.g. "OmniAccess's worldwide reach").
- Incorrect spellings such as "OMNIACCESS" (in body text), "Omniaccess" or
  "omniaccess" are branding violations and are flagged by the validator.

## Logo usage

- Two variants exist: **positive** and **negative**.
  - Positive logo → on bright, plain backgrounds.
  - Negative logo → ideally on an OmniAccess Anthracite background, or other dark
    backgrounds, as long as the logo icon stays clearly visible.
- **Minimum height: 10 mm.**
- Always replace any previous/legacy logos with the current OmniAccess logo.

## Protection zone (clear space)

- The logo must never be cluttered by competing elements. Keep a clear space around
  it, free of graphics or text.
- The clear space equals the **height of the logo icon** in the primary logo,
  measured above, below, left and right of the lettering. (To the left, measure from
  the logo icon.)
